Regjistri i Pasurive të Paluajtshme

The Regjistri i Pasurive të Paluajtshme is Albania's national real property register — maintained by the ZRPP and recording all ownership rights, mortgages, and encumbrances for Albanian real estate.

Albania's national property register is administered by the ZRPP (Zyra e Regjistrimit të Pasurive të Paluajtshme) through its network of district offices. The register records all akte notariale (notarial deeds), hipotekë (mortgages), shënim (annotations), and other entries affecting real property rights.

Registration in the regjistri is constitutive for most property rights — ownership only passes to the buyer upon registration of the akt noterial. The certifikata e pronësisë issued by the ZRPP reflects the current state of the register for that property.

Albania's register has undergone significant modernisation since the early 2000s, including digitisation of records and introduction of an online search portal. However, some historical records from rural areas or properties subject to restitution disputes may have incomplete or contested entries.

The register records not just ownership but also servitute (easements), e drejta e kalimit (rights of way), hipotekë (mortgages), and shënim (judicial notations including court orders, bankruptcies, and pending disputes). A full extract reveals all of these.

Common Mistake: Relying only on the current certifikata e pronësisë without requesting the full historik (history). Previous undischarged hipotekë, disputed transfers, or unresolved annotations may not be visible on the current-state extract alone.
Expert Tip: Request a çertifikatë pronësie me historik (certificate with full ownership history) from the ZRPP, not just the standard current-state extract. The historical view reveals any problematic entries that may have been annotated and supposedly resolved but actually remain pending.

Before any Albanian property transaction, the buyer's lawyer must obtain a full register extract (çertifikatë pronësie me historik) covering the complete ownership history of the property, not just the current entry. This reveals any gaps, disputes, or undischarged encumbrances.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does a full ZRPP register extract show in Albania?

A full extract (çertifikatë pronësie me historik) from the ZRPP shows the complete history of the property's registered entries — all ownership transfers, mortgages created and cancelled, easements, court annotations, and any current encumbrances in force.

How long does ZRPP registration take in Albania?

After submission of a valid akt noterial, the ZRPP typically completes registration of the transfer within five to fifteen working days, though this varies by district office workload. During this period, the buyer does not yet hold a certifikata in their name.

Is Albania's property register publicly accessible?

The ZRPP portal provides partial public access to register information. Full certified extracts are available on application at ZRPP district offices or through licensed Albanian lawyers and notaries.
