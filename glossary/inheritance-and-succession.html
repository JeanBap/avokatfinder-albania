Inheritance and Succession

The legal process by which property and other assets of a deceased person pass to heirs under the will or laws of succession.

Definition

Inheritance and succession refer to the legal process by which the property, assets, and liabilities of a deceased person are transferred to their heirs or beneficiaries. In Albania, succession law is governed by the Civil Code and establishes rules for who may inherit, in what order, and in what proportions. Albanian law recognizes both testamentary succession, where the deceased leaves a valid will, and intestate succession, where no will exists and the law determines the distribution of assets according to a statutory order of heirs.

In Albania, the order of succession typically gives priority to children and descendants, then to spouses, then to parents, and then to more distant relatives. The law provides for forced heirship, meaning certain family members, particularly children and spouses, are entitled to inherit a portion of the estate regardless of the testator's wishes expressed in a will. This forced heir protection ensures that close family members are not entirely disinherited. The proportion reserved for forced heirs varies depending on the number and category of heirs.

A will must comply with formal requirements to be valid in Albania, including being executed in writing, signed by the testator, and often witnessed by two witnesses. The testator must have testamentary capacity at the time of execution. Wills can be revoked or amended by the testator at any time during their life. If the testator dies without a valid will, the estate is distributed according to the statutory order of succession. Wills should be kept in a safe place, and the executor named in the will should know of its location and have a copy.

The probate process in Albania involves proving the validity of the will or establishing the legal heirs if no will exists, inventorying the estate's assets, paying debts and taxes, and distributing the remaining assets to the heirs or beneficiaries. An executor or administrator is responsible for overseeing this process. The process can be time-consuming, particularly if there are disputes over the will's validity or the classification of assets. Professional assistance from a lawyer or notary is often valuable in navigating the probate process.

Albanian succession law recognizes the rights of spouses, particularly through marital property provisions and spousal forced heir status. The surviving spouse typically has inheritance rights that depend on the number of other heirs. Additionally, succession law in Albania addresses the rights of illegitimate children, though these rights have been expanded in recent reforms to ensure more equal treatment. Understanding the applicable succession law is essential for estate planning and ensuring that assets pass as intended.

Key Facts

  • Succession in Albania follows statutory order: children, spouse, parents, distant relatives.
  • Forced heirship protects close family members from complete disinheritance.
  • Wills must be written, signed by testator, and often witnessed to be valid.
  • If no valid will exists, property is distributed according to intestate succession laws.
  • The probate process involves proving the will, inventorying assets, paying debts, and distributing to heirs.

Common Mistake

People fail to execute valid wills, leading to intestate succession and family disputes over inheritance.

Expert Tip

Execute a formal will that complies with Albanian requirements, keep it in a safe place, and inform your executor of its location.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the order of succession in Albania?

The statutory order is: children and descendants first, then spouse, then parents, then more distant relatives.

Can the testator disinherit close family members?

No, forced heirs such as children and spouses are entitled to a statutory portion of the estate regardless of the testator's wishes in the will.

What formalities are required for a valid will?

A will must be written, signed by the testator, and often witnessed by two witnesses to be valid in Albania.
