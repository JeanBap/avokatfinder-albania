Hipotekë

A hipotekë is the Albanian term for a mortgage — a security right over real property registered with the ZRPP that gives the lender enforceable priority against the property in the event of borrower default.

In Albania, a hipotekë is established by a notarial deed and registered in the ZRPP property register. Registration gives the lender a real right (e drejta reale) over the mortgaged property, effective against any subsequent purchaser. The priority of competing hipotekë is determined by order of registration.

Albanian mortgage lending is regulated by the Bank of Albania (Banka e Shqipërisë). Commercial banks offer hipotekë-backed loans for residential purchases, typically requiring a minimum 20–30% deposit. Interest rates are generally higher than Western European averages, reflecting Albania's developing financial market.

When purchasing Albanian property subject to an existing hipotekë, the purchase contract should include a condition that the mortgage is fshirë (cancelled) from the ZRPP register at closing. The notary coordinates the simultaneous loan repayment and cancellation.

After repaying an Albanian hipotekë, the lender must issue a deklaratë fshirjeje (cancellation declaration). This is submitted to the ZRPP with an application for removal of the encumbrance from the certifikata e pronësisë.

Common Mistake: Completing an Albanian property purchase without confirming that the seller's hipotekë is fully cancelled at the ZRPP before transferring funds. An uncancelled hipotekë follows the property to the new owner.
Expert Tip: Structure the Albanian purchase transaction so the portion of funds needed to discharge the hipotekë is paid directly to the lender, with the ZRPP cancellation confirmed before the balance is released to the seller.

Albania's mortgage market has grown significantly since the early 2000s, but hipotekë enforcement through Albanian courts remains slow. Buyers should assess the financial standing of any lender whose hipotekë is registered against a property they are purchasing.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a deklaratë fshirjeje in Albania?

A deklaratë fshirjeje is the lender's cancellation declaration confirming the hipotekë debt has been repaid. It must be submitted to the ZRPP along with an application for removal of the mortgage entry from the property register.

How is a hipotekë enforced in Albania?

Enforcement of an Albanian hipotekë proceeds through court-supervised auction proceedings (procedura ekzekutive). Albanian courts handle enforcement, though the process can be lengthy by Western European standards.

Is mortgage lending regulated in Albania?

Yes. The Bank of Albania regulates all mortgage lending activities. Commercial banks offering hipotekë-backed loans must comply with BKS capital and risk requirements, and LTV ratios are informally capped at 70–80% of appraised value.
