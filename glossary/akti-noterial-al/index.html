Akt Noterial

An akt noterial is an Albanian notarial deed — a document authenticated by a licensed Albanian noter (notary) that provides the highest level of legal authority for property transfers and mortgage creation.

The akt noterial (notarial deed) is the mandatory form for all Albanian real estate transfers and hipotekë creation. Albanian law requires the noter (notary public) to verify the identities of both parties, confirm the property's registered status, read the deed aloud, and certify it. Only then does the deed have legal effect for ZRPP registration purposes.

Albanian notaries are licensed by the Dhoma Kombëtare e Noterëve (National Notary Chamber) and operate as independent public officials. Their fees are regulated and calculated based on the transaction value. Parties must appear in person at the notary's office unless a power of attorney (prokurë) is used.

The noter has obligations beyond mere certification: they must conduct anti-money laundering checks, verify that the seller holds clear title in the ZRPP register, confirm the property is free of encumbrances (or that existing ones will be discharged at signing), and check for any legal restrictions on the transfer.

For foreign buyers who cannot be present in Albania, a notarised and apostilled prokurë (power of attorney) authorising a local representative to sign the akt noterial is accepted by Albanian notaries. The prokurë must be drafted carefully to include the specific transaction details.

Common Mistake: Relying on a private written agreement for Albanian property without obtaining an akt noterial. A private contract does not transfer legal title in Albania — only a notarially certified deed registered with the ZRPP constitutes a valid ownership transfer.
Expert Tip: Ask the noter to pull a fresh ZRPP extract immediately before signing the akt noterial to confirm no last-minute encumbrances have been registered. This is especially important if there has been any delay between the initial due diligence and the signing date.

After the akt noterial is signed and certified, it is submitted to the ZRPP for registration of the transfer. This registration typically takes several working days. During this period, the buyer does not yet hold registered title.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is an akt noterial required for Albanian property transfers?

Albanian law requires all real estate transfers to be executed as a notarially certified akt noterial. The noter verifies both parties' identities and the property's clear title before certification. An uncertified private contract cannot be registered with the ZRPP and does not transfer legal ownership.

Can I use a power of attorney for an Albanian property purchase?

Yes. A prokurë (power of attorney) notarised in the buyer's country and apostilled under the Hague Convention can authorise an Albanian representative to sign the akt noterial on the buyer's behalf. The prokurë must be specific to the transaction details.

How are Albanian notarial fees calculated?

Albanian notarial fees are set by a regulated tariff based on the declared transaction value. The combined total of transfer tax and notarial fees typically adds 3–5% to the purchase price, excluding ZRPP registration fees.
