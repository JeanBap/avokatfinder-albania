Tatimi mbi Pasurinë

Tatimi mbi pasurinë is the Albanian annual property tax — a recurring levy on real estate ownership payable each year to the local municipality based on the property's assessed value.

Albanian annual property tax (tatimi mbi pasurinë) is levied by and payable to the local bashki (municipality) where the property is located. The tax base is the official assessed value of the property, and the rate varies by municipality and property category (residential, commercial, agricultural).

For residential property, the annual tatimi mbi pasurinë is generally modest by Western European standards — typically ranging from a few thousand ALL to tens of thousands ALL per year depending on the property's location and size. Tirana municipality has higher rates than rural areas.

The tax is assessed annually by the municipality. Property owners receive an assessment notice (njoftim tatimor) and must pay by the specified deadline. Failure to pay results in penalties and interest. Unpaid tatimi mbi pasurinë can become a lien on the property — buyers should verify no outstanding tax debts before purchase.

At the time of purchasing Albanian property, the buyer should request a deklaratë pa detyrime (declaration of no outstanding obligations) from the local municipality confirming no outstanding tatimi mbi pasurinë or utility debts are attached to the property.

Common Mistake: Completing an Albanian purchase without requesting a deklaratë pa detyrime from the municipality. Unpaid tatimi mbi pasurinë from previous owners can become the new owner's liability or result in a lien on the property.
Expert Tip: Appoint an Albanian accountant or property manager to monitor and pay the annual tatimi mbi pasurinë on your behalf. The municipality's notification system is not always reliable for non-resident owners.

Non-resident owners of Albanian property are equally subject to tatimi mbi pasurinë. The municipality sends assessment notices to the registered address, which for non-residents is typically the property itself — making it advisable to appoint a local representative to monitor and pay the annual tax.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Albanian annual property tax rate?

The tatimi mbi pasurinë rate varies by municipality and property category. Rates in Tirana are higher than in rural areas. For residential property, the annual liability is typically modest but has increased in recent years as municipalities update their assessed values.

Can unpaid Albanian property tax become a lien?

Yes. Unpaid tatimi mbi pasurinë can result in the municipality registering a charge against the property. Buyers should request a deklaratë pa detyrime from the municipality confirming no outstanding taxes before completing the purchase.

How do non-resident Albanian property owners pay the annual property tax?

Non-residents must pay tatimi mbi pasurinë to the local bashki annually. Many appoint a local Albanian representative (person or agency) to receive tax notices, make payments, and handle any compliance correspondence on their behalf.
