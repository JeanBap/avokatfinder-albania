Certifikata e Pronësisë

The certifikata e pronësisë (certificate of ownership) is the official Albanian title document issued by the ZRPP confirming a person's registered ownership of real property.

In Albania, the certifikata e pronësisë is issued by the Zyra e Regjistrimit të Pasurive të Paluajtshme (ZRPP — Real Estate Registration Office) upon registration of a property transfer. It serves as the primary proof of legal ownership and is required for any subsequent sale, mortgage, or encumbrance.

The certificate contains the property identifier (numri i pasurisë), owner's details, property description, area, and any registered encumbrances. It must be presented to the notary at any future transaction to confirm the owner's title. Lost certificates can be replaced by the ZRPP upon application.

Albania underwent a significant property restitution and legalisation programme (ALUIZNI) following the post-communist transition. Many properties were built informally and have required legalisation before a certifikata could be issued. Buyers should confirm the property has a fully legalised certifikata before purchasing.

The ZRPP maintains a digital register accessible through its online portal. Buyers can verify the existence and status of a certifikata for any property by searching the register using the property's cadastral reference number.

Common Mistake: Accepting a seller's certifikata without independently verifying it against the ZRPP register. Fraudulent certificates have been produced in Albania — always cross-reference with the official register.
Expert Tip: Request a fresh ZRPP extract (çertifikata nga regjistri) on the day of the notarial signing, not a copy provided by the seller. The fresh extract shows the current registered owner and any encumbrances.

Always verify that the certifikata e pronësisë shown by the seller matches the current ZRPP register entry — fraudulent or outdated certificates have been used in Albanian property fraud schemes targeting foreign buyers.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the ZRPP in Albania?

ZRPP stands for Zyra e Regjistrimit të Pasurive të Paluajtshme — the Albanian Real Estate Registration Office. It maintains the national property register and issues certifikata e pronësisë documents confirming registered ownership.

What is ALUIZNI in Albanian property law?

ALUIZNI is the Albanian Agency for Legalisation, Urbanisation and Integration of Informal Zones/Constructions. It administered the legalisation of informally built properties, enabling their formal registration with the ZRPP and the issuance of certifikata e pronësisë.

How do I verify a property's title in Albania?

Search the ZRPP online portal using the property's numri i pasurisë. The register shows the current registered owner, property description, and any mortgages or encumbrances. Your Albanian lawyer can obtain an official extract directly from the ZRPP office.
