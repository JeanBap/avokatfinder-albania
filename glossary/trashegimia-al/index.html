Trashëgimia e Pasurisë

Trashëgimia e pasurisë refers to property inheritance in Albania — the legal process by which real estate ownership passes to heirs upon the death of the registered owner, governed by the Albanian Civil Code.

Albanian inheritance law follows a civil law model based on the Civil Code. Intestate succession (trashëgimi ligjore) distributes property among heirs in a fixed priority order: surviving spouse and children first, then parents, then siblings. A valid will (testament) can alter this distribution within the limits set by mandatory share (pjesa e detyruar) rules.

The mandatory share (pjesa e detyruar) guarantees certain close relatives — children, parents — a minimum proportion of the estate regardless of the will's contents. For example, children are generally entitled to half of what they would have received under intestate succession even if disinherited by will.

To transfer Albanian inherited property into the heirs' names, the heirs must obtain a vendim trashëgimie (inheritance decree) from the Gjykata e Rrethit Gjyqësor (District Court) or a deklaratë trashëgimie (inheritance declaration) certified by a noter. This document is then registered with the ZRPP to update the certifikata e pronësisë.

Inherited Albanian property that was subject to communist-era nationalisation may still be subject to pending restitution claims by the original owner's heirs. Buyers of such property face the risk of competing claims unless the restitution history has been fully resolved.

Common Mistake: Purchasing Albanian property from heirs without verifying that all heirs have signed the notarial transfer deed. Albanian law requires all co-heirs to consent to a sale — a purchase from only some heirs can be challenged by the others.
Expert Tip: Before buying inherited Albanian property, ask your lawyer to verify the complete inheritance proceedings — including the vendim trashëgimie, confirmation of all registered heirs, and that no competing restitution claims are pending against the property.

Foreign heirs of Albanian property must follow the same legal process as Albanian nationals but may face practical difficulties dealing with Albanian courts remotely. Local legal representation is essential for efficient administration of an Albanian estate.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a vendim trashëgimie in Albania?

A vendim trashëgimie is a court inheritance decree issued by an Albanian District Court confirming the heirs' entitlements to a deceased person's estate. It is submitted to the ZRPP to register the transfer of property from the deceased owner to the heirs.

What is the mandatory inheritance share in Albania?

Albanian law protects certain relatives through the pjesa e detyruar (mandatory share). Children are generally entitled to half of what they would have received under intestate rules, even if a will attempts to disinherit them.

Can a foreigner inherit Albanian property?

Yes. Foreign nationals may inherit Albanian property under Albanian civil law on the same basis as Albanian nationals. However, foreign heirs may need to obtain recognition of their status through an Albanian court if their connection to the deceased is not documented in Albanian records.
