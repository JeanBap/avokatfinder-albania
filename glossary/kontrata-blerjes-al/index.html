Kontratë Blerje-Shitjeje

A kontratë blerje-shitjeje is the Albanian property purchase and sale agreement — the preliminary or final written contract setting out the terms agreed between buyer and seller before or at the notarial signing stage.

In Albanian property transactions, the kontratë blerje-shitjeje typically serves as either a preliminary agreement (para-kontratë) setting out heads of terms before the formal akt noterial is prepared, or is used interchangeably to refer to the substantive purchase deed itself. A preliminary kontratë is binding on both parties but does not transfer title.

A preliminary kontratë blerje-shitjeje typically includes the agreed purchase price, the payment schedule, the deposit amount (kapara), the timeframe for completing the final notarial deed, and conditions precedent such as mortgage approval. Both parties sign, and a copy is retained by each.

The kapara (deposit/earnest money) paid at signing of the preliminary kontratë functions similarly to the foglaló in Hungary — if the buyer withdraws, the kapara is forfeited. If the seller withdraws, they typically owe double the kapara to the buyer. This principle is established by the Albanian Civil Code.

A preliminary kontratë can be registered with the ZRPP to protect the buyer's priority during the period between signing the preliminary agreement and completing the final akt noterial. This registration prevents the seller from encumbering or selling the property to a third party during the interim period.

Common Mistake: Treating a signed preliminary kontratë as equivalent to registered ownership. In Albania, only the akt noterial registered with the ZRPP transfers legal title — a kontratë is a contractual obligation, not a property right.
Expert Tip: Register the preliminary kontratë with the ZRPP immediately after signing. This annotation prevents the seller from double-selling or mortgaging the property while the final notarial deed is being prepared.

Foreign buyers in Albania often sign a preliminary kontratë remotely while arranging Albanian legal representation and financing, with the formal akt noterial signed during a subsequent visit or via prokurë.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a kapara in Albanian property law?

Kapara is an earnest money deposit paid at signing of the preliminary kontratë. Under the Albanian Civil Code, if the buyer defaults, the kapara is forfeited to the seller. If the seller defaults, they must repay double the kapara to the buyer.

Can a preliminary Albanian property contract be registered?

Yes. A preliminary kontratë blerje-shitjeje can be registered with the ZRPP, creating an annotation (shënim) on the property's register entry. This protects the buyer's priority by preventing the seller from transferring or encumbering the property during the interim period.

What happens if an Albanian property seller refuses to complete?

If the seller refuses to honour a signed preliminary kontratë, the buyer is entitled to double the kapara paid. The buyer may also pursue specific performance through Albanian courts, though this route is slower. Including a strong kapara clause provides the most practical protection.
