Pengim

Pengim is the Albanian term for a pledge or general charge — a security right over property (including real estate) used as collateral to secure a financial obligation other than a traditional mortgage.

In Albanian property law, pengim (pledge) is a broader security concept that includes both hipotekë (registered mortgage over real property) and pengu mbi sende të luajtshme (pledge over moveable assets). For real estate, the relevant form is the hipotekë, registered with the ZRPP, while pengim in a wider sense can also include charges over moveable property associated with a building or development.

Commercial developers in Albania sometimes use pengim arrangements to secure construction financing — pledging building materials, equipment, or receivables from pre-sale agreements as security alongside the hipotekë on the land. Buyers of off-plan units should verify what assets have been pledged to the developer's lender.

Albanian civil law governs the creation and enforcement of pengim. A pledge over real property must be registered with the ZRPP to be effective against third parties. Registration of the pengim/hipotekë gives the lender priority over the property in any subsequent insolvency or enforcement proceedings.

When purchasing from a developer who has a construction loan secured by pengim/hipotekë on the development site, buyers must ensure that the specific unit being purchased is released from the security arrangements before title is transferred. This coordination should be handled by the buyer's Albanian lawyer.

Common Mistake: Purchasing an off-plan Albanian property without verifying that the developer's lender has released the specific unit from any hipotekë/pengim on the development site. An unreleased charge can prevent the buyer from obtaining a clean title.
Expert Tip: Ask the developer's lawyer to provide a lender's release letter (letër lirimi) from the construction lender confirming that the specific unit is free from the site hipotekë before signing the purchase akt noterial.

The Bank of Albania's banking regulation requires commercial banks to register all real property collateral with the ZRPP. Informal or unregistered charges are not effective as security in Albania.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between hipotekë and pengim in Albania?

Hipotekë is specifically a registered mortgage over real property, always requiring ZRPP registration. Pengim is a broader concept covering pledges over both real and moveable property. For real estate security in Albania, hipotekë is the standard and correct term.

How is a pengim/hipotekë released in Albania?

After repayment of the secured obligation, the lender issues a deklaratë fshirjeje (cancellation declaration). This is submitted to the ZRPP with an application for removal of the pengim/hipotekë entry from the property's register.

Can an Albanian property be sold if it is subject to a pengim?

A property subject to a registered hipotekë/pengim can technically be sold, but the lender's consent or simultaneous discharge at closing is required. Most purchase contracts include a condition that all registered charges are discharged before the buyer's title is registered with the ZRPP.
