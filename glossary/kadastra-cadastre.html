Kadastra (Cadastre)

The official public register of all land parcels and their ownership in a jurisdiction.

What Is Kadastra (Cadastre)?

The Albanian Cadastre, known as kadastra, is the comprehensive public registry that documents all land parcels, their boundaries, ownership, and any encumbrances. It serves as the foundational record for property rights in Albania and is essential for legal certainty in real estate transactions. The system was modernized following independence and now operates according to EU standards.

Kadastra maintains detailed information about each property parcel, including its location, size, boundaries, and owner identity. This information is public and accessible to anyone with a legitimate interest, making it easier to verify property details before purchase or investment. The cadastral records are organized by municipality and continuously updated.

The registration of property rights in the kadastra creates presumption of ownership. This means that the person registered as owner in the cadastre is legally presumed to be the true owner unless proven otherwise. This protection encourages investment and facilitates property transactions.

Kadastra also records restrictions and encumbrances on property, such as mortgages, easements, and usufruct rights. These registrations provide notice to potential buyers and creditors about limitations on the property. The transparency helps prevent disputes and fraud in real estate dealings.

Access to cadastral information has been significantly improved through digitization in recent years. Property owners and interested parties can now obtain certified extracts and verify information more quickly. Online access to cadastral data has enhanced transparency and reduced bureaucratic delays.

Key Facts

  • Official public register of all land parcels and ownership
  • Maintained by the Real Estate Registration Office
  • Registration creates legal presumption of ownership
  • All encumbrances and restrictions are recorded
  • Information is publicly accessible for verification purposes

Common Mistake

Believing that oral agreements or possession alone establish legal ownership without cadastral registration. In Albania, property rights must be registered in the kadastra to have full legal effect.

Expert Tip

Obtain a certified cadastral extract before signing any property purchase agreement. This extract confirms the current owner, any existing mortgages, and other encumbrances. It takes only a few days to obtain and provides crucial due diligence.

FAQs

Who maintains the Albanian cadastre?

The Real Estate Registration Office operates and maintains the cadastre on behalf of the Albanian government.

Can I access cadastral information about someone else's property?

Yes, cadastral information is public. You can request an extract for any property parcel.

What if cadastral records contain errors?

You can file a correction request with the Real Estate Registration Office. Errors can often be corrected through an administrative process.
