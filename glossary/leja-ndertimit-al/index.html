Leje Ndërtimi

A leje ndërtimi is an Albanian building permit — the official authorisation issued by the local municipality permitting the construction or substantial modification of a building on a specific plot of land.

The leje ndërtimi (building permit) is required before any new construction or major structural alteration in Albania. It is issued by the bashki (municipality) after review of architectural plans, site surveys, and compliance with the urban regulatory plan (plani rregullues). Commencing construction without a permit is illegal.

Albania's post-communist era saw widespread construction without permits — the ALUIZNI legalisation programme addressed much of this legacy. However, properties built or extended after the ALUIZNI cut-off date without permits remain illegal constructions and cannot be sold with clear title until legalised.

Buyers of Albanian property should verify that the building on the purchased land matches the leje ndërtimi and the certifikata e pronësisë. Extensions, extra floors, or structural changes made without permits create legal risk and may require costly demolition or a new legalisation process.

The Inspektorati Kombëtar i Mbrojtjes së Territorit (IKMT — National Territorial Inspection Inspectorate) monitors compliance with building permits. IKMT has authority to order demolition of unauthorised structures, including those built by previous owners.

Common Mistake: Purchasing Albanian property without checking whether any structures on the land have valid leje ndërtimi. Unauthorised constructions discovered post-purchase can be subject to IKMT demolition orders that the new owner inherits.
Expert Tip: Request copies of the leje ndërtimi and any subsequent modification permits from the seller. Cross-reference the permitted footprint with the actual building on site. If there are discrepancies, consult an Albanian lawyer before proceeding.

For off-plan purchases of new builds in Albania, the developer must hold a valid leje ndërtimi before accepting deposits. Buyers should request a copy of the permit and verify it with the issuing municipality before committing to an off-plan purchase.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is IKMT in Albania?

IKMT is the Inspektorati Kombëtar i Mbrojtjes së Territorit — Albania's National Territorial Inspection Inspectorate. It enforces compliance with building permits and urban planning rules, including issuing demolition orders for unauthorised structures.

What happens if an Albanian building does not have a leje ndërtimi?

An unpermitted Albanian building cannot be sold with clear title unless it qualifies for and completes the ALUIZNI legalisation process. Unpermitted structures risk IKMT demolition orders, fines, and difficulties obtaining mortgage financing.

How do I verify an Albanian building permit?

Request a copy of the leje ndërtimi from the seller and verify it directly with the issuing bashki (municipality). The permit should match the property's current state — any additions or modifications require their own permits or legalisation.
