Tatimi mbi Kalimin e Pronësisë

Tatimi mbi kalimin e pronësisë is the Albanian real estate transfer tax — a tax levied on property transactions based on the declared value or the tax-assessed value of the property, whichever is higher.

Albania levies a transfer tax on property sales. The rate varies by property type and municipality — for residential property in urban areas, the effective tax burden including all registration fees typically amounts to 3–5% of the transaction value. The tax is administered by the Administrata Tatimore e Shqipërisë (ATA — Albanian Tax Administration).

The tax base is the higher of the declared purchase price or the reference value set by the ATA for the property's zone and type. If the declared price falls below the ATA reference value, tax is assessed on the higher reference figure. This prevents under-declaration of prices to minimise tax.

In addition to the transfer tax, ZRPP registration fees apply — these are calculated based on the property value and are payable at the time of registration. The combined tax and fee burden should be budgeted for at the outset of any Albanian property transaction.

Albania has periodically revised its transfer tax rates and registration fees as part of broader fiscal reform. Buyers should verify current rates with an Albanian lawyer or the ATA before committing to a transaction, as rates change more frequently than in more established markets.

Common Mistake: Declaring a purchase price below the ATA's reference value to reduce transfer tax. The ATA will assess tax on the reference value regardless, and the under-declaration may trigger a tax audit and penalties.
Expert Tip: Ask your Albanian lawyer to obtain the ATA's current reference value for the specific property before signing. This tells you the minimum tax base and allows you to accurately budget total acquisition costs.

Certain Albanian property transactions are exempt from transfer tax, including transfers between first-degree relatives (parents and children) and certain government-programme transactions. Documentation must be submitted to the ATA to claim exemption.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who pays the transfer tax in Albania?

The transfer tax is formally the seller's obligation in Albanian law, but it is frequently agreed contractually that the buyer bears the cost or that the tax is split. The purchase agreement should specify clearly which party is responsible.

Are family property transfers exempt from Albanian transfer tax?

Transfers between first-degree relatives (parents to children and vice versa) are generally exempt from the Albanian transfer tax. Exemption must be claimed by submitting appropriate documentation to the ATA — it is not automatic.

What is the ATA reference value used for in Albania?

The ATA (Albanian Tax Administration) sets reference values for different property zones and types across Albania. These serve as the minimum tax base for transfer tax purposes — if the declared price is below the reference value, tax is assessed on the higher reference figure.
