Servitut

A servitut is an Albanian easement — a real right burdening one property (the servient estate) in favour of another property or person, typically granting rights of access, passage, or use over the burdened land.

Servitute (easements) in Albanian property law are governed by the Civil Code. Common types include servitut kalimi (right of way — allowing access across another's land), servitut ujësjellësi (water pipeline easement), and servitut drite (right of light). Easements can be established by agreement, court order, or long usage.

An easement established by agreement must be recorded in an akt noterial and registered with the ZRPP to be effective against third parties. Unregistered easements may still be enforceable between the original parties but do not bind subsequent purchasers who acquire without notice.

Buyers of Albanian property must check the ZRPP register for any registered servitute burdening the property. An undetected right of way across agricultural land, for example, can significantly affect the property's development potential and value.

Easements can also arise by long user (domosdoshmëria) — where a property is landlocked and can only be accessed across a neighbour's land, the Civil Code provides for a compulsory easement to be created by court order if the parties cannot agree. The landlocked owner compensates the neighbour for the easement.

Common Mistake: Buying Albanian agricultural or rural property without physically inspecting for informal access paths, water channels, or other uses by neighbours that may have established unregistered servitute through long usage.
Expert Tip: Request a çertifikatë me historik from the ZRPP and conduct a physical site inspection to identify any signs of neighbour usage that could indicate unregistered easements. Both steps are needed — the register may not capture everything.

When purchasing Albanian property in rural or peri-urban areas, physical inspection is particularly important for identifying informal paths, water channels, or access routes that may have created unregistered easements through long usage.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a servitut kalimi in Albania?

A servitut kalimi is an Albanian easement of passage — a real right allowing the holder to cross another's property. It is commonly needed where a property has no direct road access. It must be registered with the ZRPP to bind subsequent owners of the burdened property.

Can an Albanian easement be removed?

An Albanian servitut can be terminated by mutual agreement of both property owners (documented by akt noterial and registered with ZRPP), or by court order if the easement is no longer necessary or has been misused. Abandonment alone is not sufficient — formal deregistration is required.

What is a domosdoshmëri easement in Albania?

Domosdoshmëri refers to necessity — under the Albanian Civil Code, where a property is completely landlocked, the owner can apply to a court to establish a compulsory servitut kalimi across a neighbouring property. The landlocked owner pays compensation to the burdened neighbour.
