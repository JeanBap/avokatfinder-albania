Hipotekë (Albanian Mortgage)

A registered charge over Albanian real property securing a loan obligation, recorded in the State Cadastre Agency register and requiring formal cancellation upon repayment.

A hipotekë in Albanian law is a security interest registered against real property to secure a debt, most commonly a bank loan. When an Albanian bank or other lender provides finance secured on property, a hipotekë is registered with the State Cadastre Agency (ASK) against the property title. This registration is what gives the lender legal priority over the property if the borrower defaults. The hipotekë appears on the certifikatë pronësie (cadastral certificate) and any buyer or third party can see that the property is encumbered.

For property buyers in Albania, discovering a hipotekë on the certifikatë pronësie is a serious matter that must be resolved before completion. The seller must either repay the underlying loan before the sale and obtain a cancellation certificate (certifikatë e fshirjes), or arrangements must be made for the purchase proceeds to simultaneously discharge the mortgage. Your avokat (lawyer) and the notary (noter) must coordinate with the lender to ensure the hipotekë is cancelled at the moment of transfer. If a property is purchased without clearing an existing hipotekë, the buyer may find the lender can enforce the charge against them as new owner.

Albanian law recognises both first-rank and lower-rank hypothecations (hipoteka e rendit të parë, të dytë). The rank determines priority in enforcement: a first-rank hipotekë holder has the first claim on proceeds if the property is sold in enforcement proceedings. Multiple hipoteka can exist on the same property, each registered in order of priority. Always verify not just whether a hipotekë exists, but also its rank and the outstanding balance, to ensure it can be discharged from the purchase price.

Obtaining a bank mortgage (hipotekë bankare) as a foreign buyer in Albania is theoretically possible but practically challenging. Most Albanian banks require Albanian tax registration, proof of income in Albania or recognised foreign income, and a permanent address in Albania. Some international or regional banks operating in Albania have more flexible criteria for EU or US nationals. Mortgage rates in Albania have historically been higher than Western European rates, reflecting the higher perceived risk environment. Many foreign buyers finance Albanian property purchases with equity from their home country.

The cancellation of a hipotekë after repayment requires a formal lender declaration (deklaratë e fshirjes) and application to the State Cadastre Agency. The lender must issue this declaration promptly after repayment, though in practice delays can occur. If a lender unreasonably delays issuing the cancellation, Albanian courts have jurisdiction to order the cancellation. Always retain your repayment receipts and correspondence as evidence. The cancellation is only effective upon registration with ASK, not upon the lender's internal loan closure.

Key Facts

  • Registered with State Cadastre Agency (ASK) and visible on certifikatë pronësie
  • Must be formally cancelled (fshirje) before a clean property transfer
  • Multiple hipoteka can exist, ranked by priority of registration
  • Foreign buyers face practical challenges obtaining Albanian bank mortgages
  • Cancellation requires lender declaration and formal ASK registration

Common Mistake

Buyers sometimes accept a verbal or informal promise from the seller that a mortgage will be cleared after the sale, without ensuring proper legal mechanisms are in place. If the seller uses the purchase proceeds to clear the mortgage but the cancellation is delayed or never completed, the buyer owns a property with an unresolved charge. Always require simultaneous cancellation at the notarial signing, coordinated by your avokat and noter.

Practical Tip

Before signing any preliminary agreement, verify the outstanding balance on any existing hipotekë directly with the registering lender, not only from seller-provided documents. Request a bank-certified statement of the outstanding balance (gjendje e detyrimeve) valid as of the transaction date. Ensure the purchase price is sufficient to discharge all encumbrances after accounting for selling agent fees and transfer taxes.

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens if I buy Albanian property without clearing the seller's mortgage?

The hipotekë remains registered against the property and is binding on you as the new owner. The lender retains the right to enforce the charge against the property even though you did not take out the loan. In the worst case, the bank can initiate enforcement proceedings and the property could be sold to recover the debt. Always ensure the mortgage is cleared before or simultaneously with the transfer.

How do I know if a property has a hipotekë?

Request an official certifikatë pronësie from the State Cadastre Agency (ASK). The document will list all registered encumbrances in the encumbrance section. Do this immediately before the notarial signing as new registrations can appear at any time.

Can I get an Albanian mortgage as a foreigner?

It is possible but difficult. Most Albanian banks require Albanian residency, tax registration, and local income evidence. Some regional banks with operations in both Albania and the EU have more flexible criteria. Many foreign buyers use equity from abroad rather than local financing. Consult with several banks before assuming a local mortgage is unavailable.
