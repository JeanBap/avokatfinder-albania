Bashkëpronësia

Bashkëpronësia is Albanian co-ownership — a legal arrangement in which two or more persons jointly own real property, each holding an undivided proportional share in the whole.

Bashkëpronësia (co-ownership) arises commonly in Albanian property through inheritance (where multiple heirs share the estate), joint purchase, or marital property. Each co-owner holds a proportional share (pjesë ideale) but no co-owner has exclusive rights to any physical part of the property without a partition (ndarje).

Albanian law distinguishes between bashkëpronësia e zakonshme (ordinary co-ownership — divisible by agreement or court order) and bashkëpronësia e detyruar (forced co-ownership — of common areas in apartment buildings which cannot be separately sold).

A co-owner can sell their share of Albanian property without the consent of other co-owners, but the other co-owners have a right of pre-emption (e drejta e parablerjes) — they must be offered the share first at the same price and terms. Only if they decline can the share be sold to a third party.

In apartment buildings (pallate), the bashkëpronësia e detyruar applies to common areas such as staircases, lifts, rooftop, and land. These cannot be sold separately from individual units. All owners contribute to maintenance costs in proportion to their unit's share.

Common Mistake: Purchasing from one co-owner without verifying whether other co-owners have been offered the right of pre-emption. A sale made without respecting the e drejta e parablerjes can be challenged by other co-owners and set aside by an Albanian court.
Expert Tip: Before purchasing a property held in bashkëpronësia, ask your Albanian lawyer to obtain written waivers of the e drejta e parablerjes from all other co-owners. Only then proceed with the akt noterial.

Purchasing from a property held in bashkëpronësia requires all co-owners' signatures on the akt noterial. If any co-owner cannot be located or refuses, the transaction cannot proceed without a court order — making inherited properties with multiple heirs particularly complex to purchase.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is e drejta e parablerjes in Albanian co-ownership?

E drejta e parablerjes is the right of pre-emption held by co-owners of Albanian property. If one co-owner wishes to sell their share to a third party, they must first offer it to the other co-owners at the same price. Only if the co-owners decline can the share be sold externally.

Can Albanian co-owned property be partitioned?

Yes. Albanian bashkëpronësia e zakonshme (ordinary co-ownership) can be partitioned by agreement between the co-owners (certified by a noter and registered with ZRPP) or by court order if the co-owners cannot agree. Forced co-ownership of apartment commons (bashkëpronësia e detyruar) cannot be partitioned.

What happens if a co-owner refuses to sell Albanian property?

If a co-owner of Albanian property refuses to consent to a sale, the property cannot be sold as a whole without their agreement. The willing seller can only sell their proportional share. Alternatively, any co-owner can petition an Albanian court for a ndarje (partition) of the property.
